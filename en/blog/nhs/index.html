Our Thesis:

The Treaty of Nanjing marked the end of the Opium war after Britain’s military overpowered China in 1842. Further pressure from Britain resulted in difficulties for Chinese diplomats attempting to negotiate fair terms. Misinterpretations of the treaty along with the emperor’s ineptitude hindered further negotiations after the first draft of the treaty. These factors led to major losses for China and marked the beginning of a century of mistreatment from other imperial powers.
